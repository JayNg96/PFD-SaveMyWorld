Reduce, Reuse and Recycle

The 3Rs play a crucial role by preventing waste generation at its source and bringing along numerous benefits.

Reduce

The best way to manage waste is to avoid producing it in the first place. This can be accomplished by shopping carefully and adhering to a few guidelines:

  • When printing materials, always make two-sided copies.
  • Purchase in bulk. Larger, economy-size products require less packaging.
  • Avoid using disposable items like paper plates, cups, napkins. Throwaways contribute to the problem and increase costs because they must be replaced repeatedly.

    • Reuse

    Reusing products makes both economic and environmental sense. Creativity is sometimes required! Reusing products for the same purpose is a good idea. Recycle paper and plastic bags, and fix broken appliances, furniture, and toys. Old clothes, appliances, toys, and furniture can be sold at garage sales or donated to charities. Bring own containers or say “no” to unnecessary plastic when getting takeaway food.

    Recycle

    Recycling is the process of converting waste materials into new materials and objects, and we can start by:

  • Reusing products makes both economic and environmental sense. Creativity is sometimes required!

  • Reusing products for the same purpose is a good idea. Recycle paper and plastic bags, and fix broken appliances, furniture, and toys.

  • Old clothes, appliances, toys, and furniture can be sold at garage sales or donated to charities.

  • Bring own containers or say “no” to unnecessary plastic when getting takeaway food.
