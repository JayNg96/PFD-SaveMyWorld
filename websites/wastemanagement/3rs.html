Reduce, Reuse & Recycle

Reduce

The best way to manage waste is to avoid producing it in the first place.

This can be accomplished by shopping carefully and adhering to a few guidelines:

  1. When printing materials, always make two-sided copies.
  2. Purchase in bulk. Larger, economy-size products require less packaging.
  3. Avoid using disposable items like paper plates, cups, napkins. Throwaways contribute to the problem and increase costs because they must be replaced repeatedly.

Reuse

Reusing products makes both economic and environmental sense. Creativity is sometimes required!

  1. Reusing products for the same purpose is a good idea. Recycle paper and plastic bags, and fix broken appliances, furniture, and toys.
  2. Old clothes, appliances, toys, and furniture can be sold at garage sales or donated to charities.
  3. Bring own containers or say “no” to unnecessary plastic when getting takeaway food.

Recycle

Recycling is the process of converting waste materials into new materials and objects, and we can start by:

  1. Purchasing recycled-material products. Look for the recycling symbol or ask the sales persons.
  2. Checking with the collection centers to see what materials they accept before beginning to collect those materials, such as metal cans, newspapers, paper products, and plastics.
  3. Using recycled paper for copier paper and newsletters.
